# ThermaShift — 90-Day Client Acquisition Plan

**Strategy:** Targeted direct outreach + free thermal assessments, supported by LinkedIn technical content.

---

## Days 1-30: Build Foundation & Start Outreach

- [ ] Publish 12+ LinkedIn posts from ThermaShift page with real technical analysis (PUE math, liquid cooling ROI models, waste heat revenue calculations, ESG regulatory breakdowns)
- [ ] Build target prospect list of 100 people with specific research on each:
  - **Titles:** VP of Data Center Operations, Director of Facilities, Chief Sustainability Officer, VP of Engineering, Energy Manager
  - **Companies:** Flexential, QTS, DataBank, Aligned, Compass Datacenters, Stream Data Centers, EdgeConnex, Vantage, Iron Mountain, Digital Realty
  - **Research each:** facility locations, recent expansions, published PUE, sustainability commitments, press releases
- [ ] Send 30 hyper-personalized outreach messages via LinkedIn InMail (10-25% response rate vs 1-3% cold email)
  - Reference their specific facility, a recent expansion, or public sustainability commitment
  - Include a custom insight (e.g., "Based on your published PUE of 1.45 at your Charlotte facility, you're likely spending $X in excess cooling costs annually")
  - Offer a free 30-minute cooling efficiency review
- [ ] Join 7x24 Exchange Carolinas chapter (free or ~$25 for local events)
- [ ] Join LinkedIn Groups: Data Center Professionals, Data Center Knowledge, Green Building & Sustainable Design, ASHRAE
- [ ] Engage daily from ThermaShift page: comment on 3-5 posts from target buyers with genuine technical insight, react to 10-15 posts

---

## Days 30-60: Build Credibility Assets & Deepen Relationships

- [ ] Create one gated whitepaper: "2026 Data Center Cooling Economics: Air vs. Liquid vs. Hybrid — The Full ROI Model"
  - Gate behind email capture on thermashift.com
  - Promote on LinkedIn
  - B2B whitepapers convert 3-5% from download to sales conversation
- [ ] Submit speaking proposals to:
  - Data Center World (speakers attend free)
  - DCD>Connect (free for end-users)
  - 7x24 Exchange conferences
- [ ] Offer free 2-3 day thermal assessments to 3-5 warmest prospects
  - Deliver report showing $X00K in annual savings potential
  - This IS the sales document — 74% of buyers choose the vendor who adds value first
- [ ] Contact liquid cooling manufacturers about referral partnerships:
  - CoolIT, GRC, Motivair, LiquidCool Solutions
  - They encounter cooling problems at their clients but don't solve them — you become their referral partner
  - Partnership referrals close at 30-50%
- [ ] Follow up on all 30 outreach messages from Days 1-30
- [ ] Continue posting 3-5x/week on LinkedIn with technical depth

---

## Days 60-90: Convert & Scale

- [ ] Attend at least one industry event (7x24 regional meetup, DCD>Connect, or local data center networking event)
- [ ] Convert free assessment into paid engagement or strong case study
  - One lighthouse client name (Flexential, QTS, Digital Realty) unlocks the rest of the market
- [ ] Ask every positive interaction for 2-3 referrals (referrals close at 25-56%)
- [ ] Launch outreach round 2 using assessment results / case study as proof point
- [ ] Identify 2-3 complementary firms for ongoing referral partnerships:
  - MEP engineering firms (WSP, Jacobs, HDR, Mortenson) — overwhelmed with data center work, need thermal specialists
  - BMS/DCIM vendors (Nlyte, Sunbird, Schneider EcoStruxure)
  - Electrical contractors working on data center builds
- [ ] Evaluate subcontracting opportunities with larger EPCs who win big data center projects but need cooling specialists

## What NOT to Waste Time On

- Paid ads (LinkedIn: $8-15/click, $75-200/lead — useless at zero budget)
- Generic cold email blasts (1-3% reply, damages brand)
- Trade publication ads ($2-5K/month, low conversion)
- SEO/blogging (6-12 months to generate inbound, too slow)
- Random networking without a target list

---

## Core Principle

**Don't market your way to clients. Diagnose your way to clients.** Offer a free assessment, show the money they're losing, and convert. Every successful data center consulting firm started this way.
